Lecture course on Borel equivalence relations and group actions, University of Bristol, October-December 2019

Schedule: See the TCC schedule. Time: Thursdays 15:00-17:00. Dates: 17th October, 24th October, 29th October (Tuesday 13:00-15:00), 31st October, 14th November, 15th November (Friday 13:00-15:00), 28th November, 5th December

Abstract: We study equivalence relations induced by actions of countable groups and their comparison with respect to Borel reducibility and orbit equivalence. Topics include (a) Dye's theorem: ergodic probability preserving actions of the integers are all orbit equivalent and (b) the E_0-dichotomy, characterizing non-smooth equivalence relations. The topic connects descriptive set theory, dynamics and logic. The course requires only basic knowledge of group actions and measure theory.

Literature: Su Gao, Invariant descriptive set theory; Alexander S. Kechris, Benjamin D. Miller: Topics in orbit equivalence

Tentative lecture plan:

1-2 Standard Borel spaces, countable and hyperfinite Borel equivalence relations, actions of countable groups
3 Universal actions for fixed groups and universal countable Borel equivalence relations
4-5 Ergodic actions, Dye's theorem on ergodic p.m.p. actions of the integers
6-8 The E_0-dichotomy and variants, Borel graphs and colourings, isomorphism of countable structures
