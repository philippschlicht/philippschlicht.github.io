Lecture course on Borel equivalence relations and group actions, University of Bristol, October-December 2019

Dates and times: See the TCC schedule. Time: Mostly Thursdays 15:00-17:00. Dates: 17th October, 24th October, 29th October (Tuesday 13:00-15:00), 31st October, 14th November, 15th November (Friday 13:00-15:00), 28th November, 5th December

Abstract: We study equivalence relations induced by actions of countable groups and their comparison with respect to Borel reducibility. This is a subfield of descriptive set theory and dynamics with applications in logic, e.g. to the complexity of isomorphism for classes of countable structures.
We plan to cover (a) Dye's theorem: actions of the integers that are sufficiently nice (preserve an ergodic probability measure) are all orbit equivalent and (b) the E_0-dichotomy: a useful characterization when an equivalence relation is non-trivial, from the viewpoint of reducibility. The course requires only basic knowledge of group actions and measure theory.

Literature: Su Gao, Invariant descriptive set theory; Alexander S. Kechris, Benjamin Miller: Topics in orbit equivalence

Tentative lecture plan:

1.-2. Polish metric spaces, standard measure spaces, isomorphisms
Smooth equivalence relations, ergodicity
Finite, hyperfine and countable Borel equivalence relations
Group actions, free actions, shift actions
Feldman, Moore: Countable group actions correspond to countable equivalence relations:
Slaman, Steel: Z-actions correspond to hyperfinite equivalence relations:

3. Universal actions for a given group
Universal countable Borel equivalence relations

4.-5. Ergodic actions
Reducing E_0 to ergodic actions
Dye: All ergodic p.m.p. actions of the integers are orbit equivalent

6.-8. The E_0-dichotomy and variants
Borel graphs and colourings
Isomorphism of countable structures
