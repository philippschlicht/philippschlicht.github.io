October-December 2019: Lecture course on Borel equivalence relations and group actions, University of Bristol (TCC)

Abstract: We study equivalence relations induced by group actions and how they compare with respect to Borel reducibility. This is a subfield of descriptive set theory and dynamics. One of its applications in logic is the study of the complexity of isomorphism for various classes of countable structures. The plan is to cover (a) Dye's theorem: actions of the integers that are sufficiently nice (preserve an ergodic probability measure) are all orbit equivalent and (b) the E_0-dichotomy: a useful characterization when an equivalence relation is non-trivial, from the viewpoint of reducibility.

Prerequisites: Basic knowledge of groups and group actions; basic measure theory; notions from descriptive set theory such as Borel and analytic sets and their properties will be covered as needed.

Literature: Gao, Invariant descriptive set theory Kechris, Miller: Topics in orbit equivalence

Tentative lecture plan: 1.-2. Polish metric spaces, standard measure spaces, isomorphisms Smooth equivalence relations, ergodicity Finite, hyperfine and countable Borel equivalence relations Group actions, free actions, shift actions Feldman, Moore: Countable group actions correspond to countable equivalence relations: Slaman, Steel: Z-actions correspond to hyperfinite equivalence relations: 3. Universal actions for a given group Universal countable Borel equivalence relations 4.-5. Ergodic actions Basic properties Reducing E_0 to ergodic actions Dye: All ergodic p.m.p. actions of the integers are orbit equivalent 6.-8. The E_0-dichotomy and variants Borel graphs and colourings Isomorphism of countable structures
