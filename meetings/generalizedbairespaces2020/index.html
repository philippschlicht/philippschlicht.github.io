Fifth Workshop on Generalised Baire Spaces, 3rd-4th of February 2020, University of Bristol

The Fifth Workshop on Generalised Baire Spaces will take place on the 3rd-4th February 2020 (Monday 9am to Tuesday 4pm) in LG.22 (Lower Ground Floor Seminar Room) at the School of Mathematics of the University of Bristol, Fry Building, Woodland Road, BS8 1UG, UK.

The meeting is open to everyone, with a conference fee of 20 GBP for those participating in the whole meeting. If you intend to participate, please send an email to philipp.schlicht@bristol.ac.uk by the 17th of January. If you plan to give a contributed talk of 20-25 minutes, please send an email soon to secure a slot.

Description Generalized descriptive set theory studies spaces of functions on uncountable regular cardinals and properties of their definable subsets. This is a relatively new and very active field of set theoretic research that has attracted much interest. For an introduction, we refer to this or this paper. This field was the theme of four previous workshops in Amsterdam in November 2014, Hamburg in September 2015, Bonn in September 2016 and Amsterdam in August 2018. Additionally, a symposium took place in Prague in August 2019.

The aim of this workshop is to bring together several research groups and outline directions of future research. The focus of this year's workshop is on connections with model theory and infinite combinatorics.

Main speakers David Aspero (Norwich), Vincenzo Dimonte (Udine), Lorenzo Galeotti (Amsterdam), Philipp Lücke (Bonn), Miguel Moreno (Vienna), Luca Motto Ros (Torino), Isabel Müller (London), Sarka Stejskalova (Prague), and Dorottya Sziraki (Budapest).

Contributed talks Vera Fischer (Vienna), Radek Honzik (Prague) (slots available)
